# Competitive Critical Review (April 2026)

This review is intentionally blunt. It focuses on where Tarka is behind, where it is competitive, and what must change to win against market leaders.

## Bottom line

- Tarka is strong for teams that need openness, extensibility, and vendor-neutral control.
- Tarka is not yet best-in-class for managed intelligence network effects, mature reputation data depth, and turnkey trust workflows.
- The largest gap is not raw feature count. The largest gap is productized quality, confidence scoring maturity, and operational outcomes at scale.

## Brutal comparison by capability

### Device and session intelligence

- **Tarka today**: Good baseline signals across web and mobile, plus graph and rule fusion options.
- **Leaders**: Stronger productionized tamper/replay/MitM controls, richer confidence calibrations, and tighter SDK operational tooling.
- **Verdict**: Tarka is competitive on architecture, behind on packaged signal quality and consistency controls.

### Location and co-presence risk

- **Tarka today**: Basic geo/network features and extensible design.
- **Leaders**: Better location inference confidence and cross-surface co-location verification during high-risk auth moments.
- **Verdict**: Clear gap. This is a high-impact opportunity.

### Counter/velocity infrastructure

- **Tarka today**: Has ingredients but not yet a fully productized self-serve counter platform with robust online/offline replay workflows.
- **Leaders**: Mature counters, high-scale windows, experimentation loops, and direct rule integration.
- **Verdict**: Gap in product depth, not in technical feasibility.

### Analyst operations

- **Tarka today**: Good case and graph foundations.
- **Leaders**: Faster analyst decision loops via evidence summarization, stronger benchmark overlays, and polished workflow ergonomics.
- **Verdict**: Medium gap with strong near-term upside.

### Rule operations and governance

- **Tarka today**: Strong rule and simulation foundation, extensible controls.
- **Leaders**: More mature no-code rollout controls, safer experimentation defaults, and richer production telemetry around rule interactions.
- **Verdict**: Competitive core, behind in operational polish and guardrails.

## P0 risks if unaddressed

- Inconsistent confidence scoring across platforms reduces trust in automated actions.
- High false-positive friction risk when challenge orchestration is not tightly tuned.
- Replay/tamper signal gaps create blind spots for advanced abuse operators.
- Lack of standardized experiment guardrails can produce misleading outcomes.

## What to ship next to close the gap

1. Cross-SDK normalized inference schema and confidence tiers.
2. Replay/tamper/MitM detection baseline across mobile and web ingestion.
3. Counter windows (5m/1h/24h) with online/offline parity and replay.
4. Challenge orchestration with low-friction-first policy templates.
5. Analyst evidence summary and top-driver explainability.

## Success metrics

- False-positive rate reduction without fraud-loss regression.
- Median analyst review time reduction.
- Coverage of high-confidence integrity/location inference features.
- Replay/tamper detection precision and response latency.
